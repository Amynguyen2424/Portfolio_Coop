Overview

Primary Goal

It’s a mobile rental app that helps tenants find safer, more trustworthy housing with less stress. Users can browse verified listings, compare options, and manage one reusable rental application across multiple properties. Throughout the process, the in-app assistant Sachi explains requirements, flags potential scams, and guides users step-by-step so they feel supported and confident—not lost in paperwork and landlord messages.

2.

Understand The Problem

Metro Vancouver's rental market presents significant challenges that heavily impact newcomers and international students:

Problem statement

How might we redesign the rental search process to prevent scams and optimize efficiency between verified landlord and renter matching?

3.

Project Goal

Primary Goal

Create a mobile app that helps renters find verified listings, connect with legitimate landlords, manage applications efficiently, and understand their tenant rights, transforming an overwhelming process into a supportive, approachable experience.

4.

Our Solution

Unified Search Flow

Aggregate listings from multiple platforms and eliminate duplicates automatically, saving renters hours of browsing time.

Verification System

Trust scores and verification data help confirm legitimate landlords before applying, reducing the risk of scams.

One-Time Upload

Build your renter profile once with secure document storage and apply to multiple properties instantly.

Direct Communication

Chat with landlords, schedule viewings, and manage maintenance requests all in the app.

5.

Key Insights Gathered From Interviews

1.

Repetitive Documentation Wastes Time

Renters repeatedly upload the same documents across platforms. A centralized, reusable renter profile saves time and reduces frustration during applications.

2.

Verification Must Be Front and Center

International students lack local knowledge to spot scams. Badges, reviews, and trust scores need to be prominent on listings, not hidden.

3.

Keep the Supportive Tone Consistent

Our Shachi guide’s warm tone resonated during onboarding but faded later. It should stay present throughout flows to keep the supportive feel.

4.

Duplicate Listings Overwhelm Users

Multiple platforms show duplicates and outdated posts. Aggregating, deduplicating, and verifying listings builds confidence and saves time.

5.

Users Need Visual Match Indicators

Preference matches as text were confusing. Clear color-coded indicators and visuals make it obvious which properties fit.

6.

Application Progress Lacks Clarity

Applicants struggled to see status and next steps. Clear tracking and confirmations reduce stress throughout the process.

User Testing

Through multiple user testing interviews, we discovered both major usability gaps and opportunities for enhancement.
This feedback was essential in prioritizing changes and refining our prototype into a more user-friendly platform.

1.

Navigation Issue

Navbar icons are unclear and don't intuitively communicate it’s functions to users.

2.

Shachi - The Helper

The assistant is only genuinely helpful during onboarding but loses relevance afterward.

3.

Search Preferences

Make slider bar, check box, dropdowns, and add-ons work properly and be more intuitive.

4.

Visual Hierarchy

Information layout and card design need better organization and prioritization of key details.

5.

Notification Indicator

Have indicator to notification and updates visible on the interface.

6.

Enhancement

Match score visibility and display improvements

11.

Takeaway

Reflection

This project showed us the importance of balancing functionality with simplicity, particularly when designing for first-time renters navigating a stressful process. Our biggest challenge was making Shachi genuinely helpful without overwhelming users. We discovered that helper features must be contextual and subtle, only appearing when truly beneficial to the user. Through user testing, we uncovered blind spots we didn’t recognized in our design process. While we believed our navigation was intuitive, testers revealed that icon clarity and smooth view transitions were significant pain points. This experience reminded us that designers cannot rely solely on their own perspective and that regular user feedback is critical.
